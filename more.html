A longer story (that no one cares about)

From Athens to neuroimaging, with detours.

Born and raised in Athens, with a path that wandered through electronics, mathematics, image processing, and eventually the brain.

I completed my BA in the neighbouring city of Piraeus, where I developed a strong interest in microcontrollers and electronics. This led me to take my first steps in programming with C and Assembly. During my internship I had the opportunity to work alongside experts in the field, and for my dissertation I developed a hardware-based GUI to control a 5-DOF robot arm.

Shortly after, I briefly attended the Mathematics department at the University of Patras, taking a few courses out of personal interest — without completing the degree. It was there that I came across an interdisciplinary MA program in Electronics & Information Processing, which I decided to pursue. My master's focused on image and signal processing, data analysis, and embedded systems. A course in Biomedical Imaging introduced me to brain imaging and the neural correlates of brain function, and that was it — I was hooked.

This led me to a PhD in Neurosciences, focusing on machine learning applications in disorders of consciousness, defended in October 2019. In March 2020, I joined the Applied Machine Learning group at the Institute of Neuroscience and Medicine (INM-7) at Research Centre Jülich as a Postdoctoral Researcher, where I spent six years working on medical imaging and data analysis. Among other things, I have been trying to understand the brain aging trajectory — partly out of scientific curiosity, partly out of a personal interest in keeping my own brain as young as possible (still working on that).

Oct 2013-Oct 2019: PhD candidate in Neurosciences (Full Scholarships) University of Liege, BE
Title: Machine Learning applications for characterizing brain-damaged patients’ level of consciousness
Brief Synopsis: In my thesis I applied Machine Learning techniques on functional neuroimaging data for characterizing the level of consciousness of patients with damaged brains. The results provided important information for the neural correlates of consciousness. Additionally, a Python library for processing medical images, which can be used as a complementary tool to the behavioral assessments, was developed and validated.

More info about ME

I was born and raised in Athens, Greece, where I also went to school. I did my BA in the neighboring city of Piraeus. During my undergrad, I became fascinated with microcontrollers (μCs) and electronics, which led me to take my first steps in programming with C and Assembly. In my internship, I had the opportunity to work with experts in μCs, and for my dissertation, I developed a hardware-based GUI to control a 5-DOF robot arm. Afterward, I briefly attended the Mathematics department at the University of Patras, taking a few courses out of personal interest. It was there that I came across an announcement for an interdisciplinary MA program called "Electronics & Information Processing," which I decided to pursue. My master's studies focused on image and signal processing, data analysis, electronics and embedded systems. In the course Biomedical Imaging, I worked with medical images, and this is where brain imaging and the neural correlates caught my interest. This ultimately led me to pursue a PhD in Neurosciences and scecifically in Machine learning applications in disorders of consciousness. I defended my PhD thesis in October 2019 and few months after I started working as Post Doctoral researcher with main focus in Brain age prediction.
